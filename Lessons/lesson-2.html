Lesson 2: Poker Hand Rankings

🃏 What is a Poker Hand?

In Texas Hold’em, each player makes the best possible five-card poker hand using a combination of their two hole cards and the five community cards. The strength of these hands determines the winner at showdown.

📊 Hand Rankings (Lowest to Highest)

  • High Card – No combinations. Highest single card wins.
  • One Pair – Two cards of the same rank.
  • Two Pair – Two different pairs.
  • Three of a Kind – Three cards of the same rank.
  • Straight – Five consecutive cards, different suits.
  • Flush – Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.
  • Full House – Three of a kind + a pair.
  • Four of a Kind – Four cards of the same rank.
  • Straight Flush – Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Royal Flush – A, K, Q, J, 10 of the same suit. The best hand possible.

❗️Common Mistakes Beginners Make

  • Thinking a flush beats a full house (it doesn’t — full house is stronger).
  • Not realizing that suits have no rank in poker (spades ≠ stronger than hearts).
  • Miscounting straights (e.g., forgetting A-2-3-4-5 is a valid low straight).

💡 Tip: Use Mnemonics to Remember

A useful phrase: “High Pair Tricky Students Find Full Fours Straight Royals”

(High card, Pair, Three of a kind, Straight, Flush, Full House, Four of a Kind, Straight Flush, Royal Flush)
